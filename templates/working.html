Problem Statement

Develop an integrated system for pollution analysis in India, consolidating dispersed air, water, and noise pollution data. Utilize machine learning models to predict and compare pollution levels at state and city levels, addressing the current fragmented approach and enhancing nationwide understanding and management strategies.

Introduction

This project delves into the critical issue of air, water, and noise pollution's detrimental effects on human health and ecosystems, emphasizing the importance of understanding, analyzing, and predicting pollution levels for effective interventions. By employing advanced machine learning algorithms like Random Forest, SVM, KNN, Decision Trees, and Multi-Linear Regression, the study aims to predict pollution levels and identify areas needing immediate attention. These algorithms utilize diverse mechanisms to offer accurate predictions and insights into pollution patterns, empowering stakeholders to implement targeted interventions and guide policies for a cleaner, healthier environment and improved public health outcomes.

Objectives

To Analyze

Conduct a comprehensive analysis of air, water and noise pollution data in India

To Predict

Implement machine learning models for the prediction of air, water and noise pollution

To Compare

Evaluate and compare the performance of different machine learning models
